85. Sessions of Parliament, prorogation and dissolution.—

Effective 18/6/1951

(1) The Houses of Parliament shall be summoned to meet twice at least in every year, and The President shall from time to time summon each House of Parliament to meet at such time and place as he thinks fit, but six months shall not intervene between their its last sitting in one session and the date appointed for their its first sitting in the next session.

(2) Subject to the provisions of clause (1), the The President may from time to time—

(a) summon the Houses of either House to meet at such time and place as he thinks fit;

(b) (a) prorogue the Houses or either House;

(c) (b) dissolve the House of the People.
