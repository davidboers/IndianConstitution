241. High Courts for States in Part C of the First Schedule Union territories.—

Effective 1/11/1956

(1) Parliament may by law constitute a High Court for a State specified in Part C of the First Schedule Union territory or declare any court in any such State territory to be a High Court for all or any of the purposes of this Constitution.

(2) The provisions of Chapter V of Part VI shall apply in relation to every High Court referred to in clause (1) as they apply in relation to a High Court referred to in article 214 subject to such modifications or exceptions as Parliament may by law provide.

(3) Subject to the provisions of this Constitution and to the provisions of any law of the appropriate Legislature made by virtue of powers conferred on that Legislature by or under this Constitution, every High Court exercising jurisdiction immediately before the commencement of this Constitution the Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956, in relation to any State specified in Part C of the First Schedule or any area included therein Union territory shall continue to exercise such jurisdiction in the relation to that State or area territory after such commencement.

(4) Nothing in this article derogates from the power of Parliament to extend or exclude the jurisdiction of a High Court in any State specified in Part A or Part B of the First Schedule for a State to, or from, any State specified in Part C of that Schedule or any area included within that State Union territory or part thereof.
